❖ Common Name: Silver Oak Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Grevillea robusta

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Proteaceae

Order: Proteales

Mode of Propagation

Silver Oak can be propagated through seeds, cuttings, or tissue culture. Seed propagation is most common. Seeds are collected, sown in nurseries, and seedlings are transplanted to the field when ready. This method ensures genetic diversity.

Uses

Silver Oak is cultivated for its durable timber, used in furniture, construction, and veneers. The tree helps in carbon sequestration and supports biodiversity, making it valuable for both economic and environmental reasons.

Soil Needed

Prefers moist, well-drained loamy or clayey soils with slightly acidic to neutral pH. Thrives in high rainfall and humid environments.

Life Span

Ironwood trees are very long-lived and can survive over 200 years in optimal conditions.

Diseases and Pests

  • Pests: Rarely affected but may be attacked by aphids and scale insects.
  • Diseases: Susceptible to root rot and leaf spot in overly moist conditions.

Medical Uses

  • Antiseptic: Flower oil is used in traditional medicine to treat wounds and skin infections.
  • Respiratory Ailments: Leaves and bark help treat cough, asthma, and bronchitis.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Bark extracts relieve joint pain and inflammation.
  • Gastrointestinal Health: Seed and bark extracts are used to treat stomach issues and ulcers.
